Theoretical Foundation

Understanding the core concepts of Network Theory

Driving-Point Functions

Functions evaluated at a single port of a network. They must be Positive Real (PR) to form a physically realizable passive network.

  • Impedance Z(s): $V(s)/I(s)$
  • Admittance Y(s): $I(s)/V(s)$

Positive Real (PR) Conditions

For an LC lossless network, all poles and zeros must lay on the imaginary axis (jω-axis) and must alternate with each other seamlessly.

Why Foster and Cauer Forms?

They act as the classical standard to realize reactive network diagrams. Foster forms yield series/parallel modularity using partial fractions, while Cauer forms construct filter-like ladder networks through continued fractions.
